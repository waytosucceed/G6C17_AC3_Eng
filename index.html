Prefix


Convention/Grammar

Prefixes are word parts added to the beginning of a base word to change its meaning, such as "un-" in "unhappy" or "re-" in "redo."

Always use a hyphen if you’re using a prefix with proper nouns.

In the case of prefixes such as ‘non’ and ‘ex’, you might have to add a hyphen in between the prefix and the base word.

Words that I learnt from the story

Elapsed: Passed or gone by, especially about time.
Sentence: Three hours elapsed before the train finally arrived.

Hauled: Pulled or dragged with effort or force.
Sentence: They hauled the heavy boxes up the stairs.

Constant: Occurring continuously over some time; unchanging.
Sentence: She kept a constant watch on the children playing in the yard.
